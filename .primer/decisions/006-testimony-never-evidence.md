---
id: 006
title: Author testimony never merges into Primer's evidence
date: 2026-07-23
status: accepted
source: docs/context-contract-prd.md §4 design rules
---

**Decision:** everything an author claims (context blocks, risk areas,
verification lists) is displayed labeled as claims, verified where
possible, and can only *raise* review scrutiny — never lower it, never
appear as Primer's own finding.

**Rejected:** using author claims to skip analysis, reduce severity, or
seed unverified statements into the story.

**Why:** agent-authored claims can be confidently wrong about their own
diff; a review tool whose statements are either labeled testimony or carry
checkable citations is the durable trust position.

**Implications:** severity merges are elevation-only; unverifiable claims
become scrutiny hints, not facts.
